# I Failed, But I Feel Good: GenAI, Blogging For Fun and Touching Grass

On the 12th of Febuary, I made a blog post where I laid out my dispointment in barely blogging in 2025, how it was such a blur and 2026 was going to be `#NewYearNewMe`.

However, I wasn't going to get New Years Resolution syndrome, and slide back into old habits, so I added some accountability to the post:

> If I fail to hit the end-of-February deadline, I have to post **a clearly GenAI Hustleslop LinkedIn Status about my failure to hit the first accountability deadline of 2026**

Not only that, as took the most hallowed bond possible: I posted it to as LinkedIn status:

![LinkedIn Declaration](/images/2026/02/linked-in-declaration.png)

So did I make it? Yes and no. I managed to force something out literally at the last minute, cutting it super fine and pushing it with seconds to go:

![Github Actions showing it deployed with 10 seconds left](/images/2026/02/linked-in-declaration.png)

`Sat, 28 Feb 2026 23:59:50 GMT`

I refreshed my website... and it didn't show.

Turns out...I'd forgotten to switch it from draft... whoops!

## Accepting 

Technically... TECHICALLY! I did _blog_ three times. Just one of them was a draft..

But lets be real, that would be an lettter-of-the-law thing. And honestly, I wasn't proud of the post. It was unfocused and rambling and clearly done in a rush. I'm not happy with that and it'd leave a sour taste about blogging for the future.

So lets nuke that (feel free to go read the post from the commit if you want)[LINK TO COMMIT] and replace it with something better.

But doing the thing and failing actually helped me reset a bit. Throughout this whole thing, the stakes on this were incredibly low:

It was just for me, nothing work or family related, and my main punishment for failure is a little bit of cringe.

So I dusted my self off saturday morning and had a screen break for most of the weekend and touched some grass, including baking a cake with my kiddos.

After all that, I dusted myself off, and used the wee hours of a lazy sunday to do some self-reflection.

## First Blog: 2026 Blogging Plans

Honestly, I started off well! My [first blog](LINK TO 2026 BLOGGING PLANS) was a two-birds approach: throw in all the ideas I'd had in 2025 I'd been keeping in Todoist and grab the PR merges from the website's git repo to remind me what improvements I'd made to the site itself. 

One down!

## Second Blog: Cfgmgmtcamp 2026 Trip Report

My second post, about [Cfgmgmtcamp 2026](LINK TO CFGMGMTCAMP) started out well, as I'd already got a good base already. 

I do a ton of "Environmental Scaffolding" for conferences for years now:

- I take a ton of photos and take notes continously, so I've got a good skeleton to start with and photos to throw in for visuals or to remind myself of the important moments.
- I plan out my schedule extensively, balancing attending talks with buffer and prep time for the sessions I'm delivering.
- I write out comprehensive notes in a scratchpad format: who I saw, my gut feelings, vibes, trends I noticed, talks I attended and anything useful for future attendees, both internal (Datadog Confluence for future interested attendees and the Community Team's plans) and external (my own blog for a more general audience)

So for Cfgmgmtcamp, had a baseline version of it done, but... I wanted to elaborate and double-check on the notes I'd made for the talks I'd attended. 

That lead me into looking through the talk recordings from the Cfgmgmtcamp stream, looking through talks I hadn't managed to catch I wanted to follow up on, I started noodling with NotebookLLM, Claude, Gemini, ChatGPT and basically _massively_ increased the scope...

I also had a bit of a weird feeling in my gut around using so much GenAI for a trip report, so that was another blocker. It did lead me to find out about the [`/ai` Disclosure Manifesto](https://www.bydamo.la/p/ai-manifesto), as a backlog item I just added a note to the blog itself as a starter-for-10 before I did that.

I didn't get it merged in until the 24th, leaving only 4 days to post the last blog.

## 3rd Blog Inspiration Overload

It wasn't quite just 4 days, I'd actually gotten a few early drafts worked on on a few different topics in parralel:

- A tribute to Terrance Gore: The GOAT 1-Tool runner who died only a few weeks ago
- Staying Human in the age of AI:
- Talk about some cool and unusual flags from acros the world

I think anyone sensible can look at the huge variety across these 3 different topics and go "Ok, you're spreading yourself way too thin, pick a lane, get something short sensible and sweet out there and wrap things up!"

It didn't also help that the AI disclosure gut-feel felt extremely strong around the Terrance Gore tribute. It just felt a bit... icky, for a lack of a better word, the AI-ness bled through as I stiched together research from things like ScholarGPT, my own raw thoughts and feelings and trying to stitch it all together with Claude Code agentically.

I ended up picking _secret option 4_ and trying to cleverly talk about my approach for the whole thing, and to be fair to last-minute cramming, I _did_ get kinda close. 

I did panic and finish it up with a reference to my original vexilogy blog idea,I was wearing my Zheleznogorsk flag shirt so maybe that did some sort of subliminal trigger on me in a fight-or-flight moment...

![RUSSIAN TOWN SHIRT](/images/2026/02/russian-town-shirt.jpg)

So in the end it was a classic case of underplanning, overscoping and spreding myself too thin. 

But there was still a nagging feeling that something new was also wrapped in and it reminded me of a study I'd had in my notes since the middle of last year:

> Despite the performance benefits, participants who collaborated with gen AI on one task and then transitioned to a different, unaided task consistently reported a decline in intrinsic motivation and an increase in boredom. Across our studies, intrinsic motivation dropped by an average of 11% and boredom increased by an average of 20%. In contrast, those who worked without AI maintained a relatively steady psychological state.

> [Research: Gen AI Makes People More Productive—and Less Motivated, by Yukun Liu, Suqing Wu, Mengqi Ruan, Siyu Chen and Xiao-Yun Xie](https://hbr.org/2025/05/research-gen-ai-makes-people-more-productive-and-less-motivated)

So... you've got an ever increasing backlog of ideas, you're solving them with AI and improving the core work, but you're not scratching that dompanine loop of satisfaction when you deliver, so you think about your backlog some more, maybe you'll work on that other thing instead, or mayb that new idea comes in...

Honestly, in retrospect this was one of the various root-causes of my 2025 blur-of-a-year and lack of blogging and stretching myself too thin across multiple side-projects. That already meant I was doing a lot of wheel spinning and initial spikes but not getting to the tangible deliverable bits.

And I was not alone...

## The AI Vampire

A bunch of people have written, blogged and posted about this core concept:

- https://jkap.io/token-anxiety-or-a-slot-machine-by-any-other-name/
- https://muratbuffalo.blogspot.com/2026/02/butlers-or-architects.html?m=1&ref=danielcorin.com
- https://steve-yegge.medium.com/the-ai-vampire-eda6e4f07163

I feel like Steve Yegge's hit particularly hard because of it's naming of the core idea ("The AI Vampire") and that he's not a babe-in-the-woods when it comes to development: If this kind of FOMO + Dopamine + Agentic Flows combo can happen to him, it can happen to anyone.

## So, What Now?

I want to get things fun and engaging again.

To start with, figure out a new writing flow to break it down to a sustainable and fun way. I need to balance proper planning, getting a helping hand with LLM tools, but without those AI vampirism.

One of the areas I'll be looking into is ["Cognitive debt"](https://www.media.mit.edu/publications/your-brain-on-chatgpt/), a concept I learned Benjamin Breen's ["What is Happening To Writing"](https://resobscura.substack.com/p/what-is-happening-to-writing) substack post. 

His story of how he's been trying to grok the GenAI world as a writer helped me really think about what I'm trying to achieve and get out of writing. "Writing is thinking in public" really stuck with me from it, ultimately that's the fun and joy, it's how to unpick an idea from your brain, convert that into prose and get it in the brains of others.

For my backlog, I think to help get a lot of my various sparks and ideas, something like a [garden](https://www.danielcorin.com/garden/) would be a nice way to clear out the backlog and help scratch that itch without going into a full post.

Thinking even beyond that, I need to disconnect and touch grass! I'm normally a vicarious reader and movie watcher, and I feel like I've dropped off massively on both of those, and my powerlifting and walking has slipped noticeably.

Overall, I feel positive, I feel good! I've had a good learning exprience and a chance to refocus!
