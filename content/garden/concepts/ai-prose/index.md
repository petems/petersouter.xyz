That metaphor from [Grace Kind](https://bsky.app/profile/gracekind.net) stuck with me, but my own complaint about AI prose isn't quite the cake, it's the flavour.

LLM output drifts toward purple. Everything "stands as a testament", every topic is "nuanced", every tool is "robust" and "seamless". Nothing is small or messy or tentative. I find it fuzzy to read. The sentences sound polished but land with less weight than a paragraph written by a real person thinking out loud. There's a sameness to it, like Cozy Sr. [put it in the replies](https://bsky.app/profile/onedreamonehope.bsky.social): "the same basic flavours and consistency and design so u get tired of it pretty quickly".

I notice it most when it slips into my own writing. A draft comes back with "leverage", "valuable", "showcasing", and technically it's not wrong, but it's not mine either. It's the voice of a press release pretending to be a person.

So a few rules I'm trying to hold to, for both work docs and personal stuff:

- If a sentence sounds like it could be on a vendor landing page, rewrite it
- If I can't say the sentence out loud without wincing, it's gone
- If I'd feel weird defending every word of a paragraph as my own, I'm not done yet

The cake is fine. I just want to be honest about who made it, and I want it to taste like something I'd actually bake.
