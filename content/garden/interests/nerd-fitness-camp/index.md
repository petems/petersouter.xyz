I went to my first [Nerd Fitness Camp](https://www.nerdfitness.com/camp/) in 2015.

I'd been reading Nerd Fitness for a while (Steve Kamb's take on fitness for people who don't naturally fit the gym-rat mould). Camp was the IRL version: a few days of activities, workshops, and strangers who all found the same website.

Walking into a group of people and saying "hi, we all like the same blog" is not my natural habitat. I was quietly terrified.

It was brilliant.

Then there was a gap. Nerd Fitness took time to figure out how to make Camp work sustainably at scale without losing what made it good. I respect that, honestly. Better to pause than to run it into the ground chasing growth.

It came back, and I was there in 2021.

<!-- Add photos from 2021 camp here -->

The thing about Camp that's hard to explain is the range of people you meet. Tech workers, teachers, nurses, people who'd never been to a gym and people who competed in powerlifting. All there because they'd read something on the internet that resonated, and decided to actually show up.

Some of those people I still talk to regularly. Real friends, not just "we follow each other on Instagram" acquaintances.

One of them took me up in a Cessna over the city of Portland.

<!-- Add Cessna / Portland aerial photo here -->

I did not grow up expecting to be the kind of person who gets taken up in a small plane by someone they met at a fitness camp on the internet. And yet.

There's something about voluntarily showing up somewhere slightly outside your comfort zone that attracts a certain kind of person. Camp had a lot of them.
