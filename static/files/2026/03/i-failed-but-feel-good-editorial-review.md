# Editorial Review: I Failed, But I Feel Good: GenAI, Blogging For Fun and Touching Grass

**Date**: 2026-03-16
**Word Count**: ~1,850
**Reading Time**: ~8 minutes

## Executive Summary

This is a genuinely engaging personal essay with a strong voice — it's self-deprecating, honest, and builds to a meaningful reflection on AI-assisted writing and motivation. The main areas for improvement are spelling/grammar errors throughout (likely from the rush the post describes!), a few passages in the back half that drift into a more polished/AI-adjacent register, and one incomplete sentence that needs finishing.

## Major Issues

1. **Incomplete sentence (line 142)**: "But... actually it's just a oroborus of more work, less satisfaction and more watching AI agents do the" — this sentence cuts off mid-thought. Needs completing.

2. **Broken link (line 101)**: `[Terrance Gore]` has no URL — it's just a bare bracket reference. The bullet point also ends mid-sentence: "who had tragically passed in the middle of my" — middle of your _what_? This needs finishing.

3. **Unclosed parenthetical (line 114)**: "I probably watched maybe 8+ hours of [Gabby's Playhouse](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=jEJtRrNrDhM)" — missing the closing parenthesis that was opened earlier in the sentence.

## Verbosity & Conciseness

Generally good — the conversational style means some wordiness is intentional and works. A few spots could tighten up:

- **Line 81-82**: "I was re-checking my own notes with NotebookLLM's reports on Youtube transcription, Claude Code for drafting, Gemini 3 with it's increased 1m context for fact-checking, ChatGPT for restructuring. Each tool to speed thing up actually bogged me down." — The tool list is effective (it shows the chaotic juggling), but "Each tool to speed thing up" is grammatically off. Consider: "Each tool I added to speed things up actually bogged me down."

- **Line 112**: The sentence starting "So, like anyone sensible..." is 85+ words and packs in about four ideas (secret option 4, replaying first blog structure, meta-commentary on only 1/3 posts having a point, motivation to come back later). Consider splitting it into two sentences after "elevated planning doc)".

- **Line 164**: "proper planning using old school writing methods way away from distractions and LLM, at least to start with" — "way away from" is a bit clunky. Maybe "well away from distractions and LLMs" or just "away from distractions and LLMs".
